About Us

Our Birth

As a dedicated telephone helpline,SAHAI was started in October 2002 in response to the growing incidences of suicide in the city of Bangalore. The service was initiated with the assistance of NIMHANS,Rotary East and Medico Pastoral Association. Today it is a service provided by Medico Pastoral Association which is a psycho social rehabilitation centre for people with mental illness.

Our Partners

Medico Pastoral Association

Medico Pastoral Association has been running mental health services since 1972. It has been involved in running the suicide prevention helpline - SAHAI, since its launch on 02 October 2002. Medico Pastoral Association conducts regular public awareness campaigns on suicide prevention through workshops, seminars, plays and poster campaigns. SAHAI operates from the premises of the Medico Pastoral Association.

National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro-Sciences (NIMHANS)

Its former director, Dr.R.M.Varma inaugurated the first training programme in suicide prevention for volunteers in October 2002. A study on 'The Epidemiology of Suicide in Bangalore' by Dr. G. Gururaj and Dr.Mohan Isaac of NIMHANS was instrumental in the setting up the SAHAI Helpline.

Rotary Bangalore East

SAHAI began as a service project of Rotary Bangalore East. "We've been effective in our work at the SAHAI Helpline because of the close co-ordination between the 3 partners: Rotary Bangalore East, NIMHANS and the Medico Pastoral Association" says Rotary Bangalore East's former President, Mr.K.K.Rajagopal.
Rotary Bangalore East has won many awards in recognition of its leadership role in addressing distress. Rotary Bangalore East has also been actively involved with other public awareness building activities.

Our work

  • We listen to every caller
  • Address the problem
  • Support the caller
  • Provide referral where required
  • Create awareness

Whether you're facing problems with your family, in school, at work or with your friends. Our Counselors will give you their undivided attention and will guide you through telephone counseling. SAHAI volunteers will help and equip you with the skills needed to cope with distressing situations of life like loss, failure, broken relationships or the ever-increasing demands of today's fast-paced life.

Talking about it with families and friends or sharing your feelings may be difficult. Maybe you don’t wish to worry those who are close to you or maybe you feel that you may be misunderstood or perhaps you don’t know how to explain the way you are feeling!

That’s when you might wish to talk to the trained volunteers at SAHAI. They are available to listen to you with empathy. They can offer alternative options or suggest people who can help with specific problems.

The service is completely confidential and free.

So next time you are feeling down, it might help to call SAHAI.

Peoples Talk

  • "I work as a school teacher in Goa and have always found the pressure at the workplace too heavy for me to bear. Internal politics at the school is unpleasant and I have always run away from these situations when I am caught up in them. Unfortunately, every job has these work pressures and politics and my coping strategy has always been to resign after some months and find another job. I have been lucky so far in that I have been successful in finding new jobs that are good and pay well. However, I am really worried because I know that I cannot continue doing this each time. I found the SAHAI Helpline number during a random Google search and called them up. Taking to the counsellor there helped me gain a fresh perspective on my work-related anxieties. I have since worked on my improving communication and assertiveness skills and this has helped me in a big way. Thank you very much."

    Maria, school teacher

  • "Hailing from a small village in Andhra Pradesh, I am the first person from there to become an engineer and live and work in a corporate job in a big city. Bangalore has been home for four years now and yet I am incredibly unhappy here. I don't fit in with the mall and pub philosophy that my colleagues subscribe to. So, I am left out of their circle. Loneliness is my constant companion since I have no other friends. I am not fluent in conversing in English and also get mocked by my colleagues for this. I have to pay the EMIs for the loan I took to buy my flat and so I cannot leave my job either. My parents are very proud of me and where I have got to in life, so I couldn't share my challenges with them? I was suicidal when I began calling up the SAHAI helpline. I had counsellors who spoke to me for 2 hours during some sessions. If I had not received the kind of encouragement and advice that I did from them, I do not know if I would even be alive today. Through these counselling sessions I realised that I am not alone and that made a big difference. I understood what my strengths are and began going for English-speaking classes. I am soon beginning meditations and yoga classes too. My life is so far more enjoyable than before and I feel more positive."

    Suresh, Software professional

  • "My boyfriend and I had been together for three years now when he suddenly began avoiding my calls and stopped meeting me as much as he used to. The relationship seemed to be fading and it was breaking my heart. I hadn’t been a demanding girlfriend or asked him for an assurance of marriage. In fact, my parents disapproved of our relationship and despite that I had stayed with him. As a 27 year old, I felt I gave him the best years of my life. I was feeling helpless when I spoke to the counsellor at SAHAI. It was the first time since this phase in my relationship began that I felt grounded and calm. Thank you for listening to me without judgements and for directing my attention to all that is working well in my life. I now feel I have so much to look forward to in life and my decision to move on without resentment has been possible because of my conversations with the counsellor. I know now more than ever that I am in charge of my own life and my own happiness."

    Saloni, Analyst

  • "Overwhelmed by the circumstances in my life, I began calling SAHAI and speaking to the counsellors at the other end of the line. Counselling is an option that she refuses to consider since she believes that only people who are “mad” go to a counsellor and she fears societal judgement if she seeks help. I am trying my best to play the role of both mother and father to my children but I am increasingly stretched thin. Though I know that the counsellors cannot fix my situation, speaking about it to them has helped me immensely.
